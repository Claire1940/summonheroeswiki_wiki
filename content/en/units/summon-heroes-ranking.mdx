## S-Tier: The God-Tier Summon Masters

The heroes in this tier represent the pinnacle of power in 2026. If you manage to roll one of these, your account progression will accelerate exponentially. These units are capable of "map godhood," meaning they can handle the pressure of high-wave chaos without breaking a sweat.

### Reaper (Mythic)
Reaper is widely considered the best unit in the game. Utilizing massive scythe swings and god-tier DPS, the Reaper consumes enemy health to sustain its own power. Its AOE is large enough to cover multiple lanes, making it the ultimate tool for clearing swarms. In 2026, the Reaper remains the "meta king" that every player dreams of summoning.

### Bear Tamer (Rare)
Despite its lower base rarity, the Bear Tamer is arguably the most broken hero in the current **summon heroes ranking**. It functions as a summoner unit, calling forth bear phantoms that nuke enemy packs from a distance. Because of its unique "Bear Judgment" loop, it can stall enemies while dealing massive burst damage. It is a favorite for solo pushes and "placement kings" who want to weave through enemy barrages.

### Demon Knight (Mythic)
The Demon Knight is the ultimate combo DPS unit. It provides legendary buffs that echo across the battlefield, increasing the efficiency of nearby towers. When awakened, the Demon Knight turns any map into a "legend zone," where enemies vanish before they can even reach the first turn of the path.

### Seraph & Divine
These two units are the masters of holy and divine power. They are often used in pairs to maximize "holy routes" and "divine battlegrounds."
*   **Seraph:** Focuses on heavenly buffs and defensive utility.
*   **Divine:** Provides supreme power boosts that turn mid-tier units into temporary monsters.

A-tier units are the backbone of most successful teams. While they might not "one-shot" every boss, they provide the necessary utility and sustained damage to reach the leaderboard's upper echelons.

|---|---|---|---|

| **Captain Dreadmore** | Pirate | Cannon Support | Chaining waves and naval battlegrounds. |

They are essential for turning tough maps into manageable gem-farming grinds.

## B-Tier: Situational Picks and Mid-Game Fillers

Units in the B-tier are reliable but predictable. They are often the first units you will farm gems with, but they lack the scaling required for 2026's endgame content.

*   **Ice Mage:** Provides essential freeze effects and slows. While useful for controlling the flow of a wave, the Ice Mage lacks the damage necessary to kill enemies before they break out of the ice.
*   **Fire Mage:** Focuses on Damage Over Time (DOT). Great for early-game "fire vibes," but tanky bosses in later stages will simply ignore the flames.
*   **Archer:** A safe, long-range choice for beginners. However, the lack of AOE makes the Archer vulnerable to being looped by massive swarms of enemies.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not over-invest in B-tier units. While they are tempting to level up, your gold and fusion crystals are better spent on pushing an A-tier unit toward its five-star evolution.

## Progression Mechanics: Fusion and Stat Rerolls

A unique aspect of the 2026 meta is that rarity is not a permanent ceiling. Through the use of **Fusion Crystals**, any unit can theoretically be elevated to a higher power level.

### How Fusion Works
Unlike other gacha games that require exact duplicates, Summon Heroes allows you to fuse units of the same rarity. For example, to evolve an Epic unit, you simply need three other Epics of any kind and a Fusion Crystal. This allows you to turn "trash" rolls into fuel for your S-tier monsters.

### The Value of Gold
In many tower defense games, gold becomes useless. In Summon Heroes, gold is a vital resource for **Stat Rerolls**. Instead of spending rare gems, you can use coins to optimize your hero's attack speed, range, and damage. This makes every story mode run valuable, as you earn gold even when replaying stages you have already cleared.

## Advanced Strategies for Leaderboard Dominance

To truly master the **summon heroes ranking**, you must look beyond individual unit power and focus on battlefield management.

*   **Target Prioritization:** You can click on specific monsters to force your heroes to prioritize that target. This is crucial during boss fights where small "leak" mobs might otherwise distract your high-DPS units like the Reaper.
*   **Trait Rerolling:** Traits are harder to come by than stats but offer game-changing modifiers. Always aim for traits that complement a hero's natural role—for example, "Speed" on an Ice Mage or "Pure Damage" on a Bear Tamer.
*   **PvP Free-For-All:** The PvP system teleports players into a central circle for a chaotic battle. In this mode, AOE units are king. Position your Demon Knight or Reaper in the center to maximize the number of targets hit during the initial teleport.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most important unit in the current summon heroes ranking?**

A: Currently, **Reaper** is the undisputed king of the ranking due to its massive AOE and health-consumption mechanics. However, **Bear Tamer** is the best "budget" unit for players who haven't rolled a Mythic yet.

**Q: How do I get more gems for summoning?**

A: You receive 50 gems every time you replay a story mode stage. Additionally, keep an eye out for hidden chests scattered throughout the seven story worlds, as these often contain gems and Fusion Crystals.

**Q: Is it better to have one S-tier hero or a full team of A-tier heroes?**

A: In the early-to-mid game, a single S-tier hero like the Reaper can carry you through most content. However, for high-level infinite maps, you will need the synergy provided by a full team of A-tier and S-tier units to handle different enemy types.

**Q: What should I do with my duplicate units?**

A: Do not sell them! Use them as "fodder" for Fusion. Since you don't need identical units to evolve, every duplicate is a stepping stone toward making your main hero a five-star powerhouse.
