## S-Tier: The Gods of the Battlefield

Units in the S-tier of our **summon hero tier** list are considered game-breaking. They possess unique mechanics that allow them to scale indefinitely or provide such massive AOE damage that they can solo entire lanes on high-difficulty maps.

### Reaper
The Reaper remains the undisputed king of DPS in 2026. Utilizing massive scythe swings, this unit doesn't just damage enemies; it consumes them. The Reaper's primary strength lies in its ability to scale its power as it clears waves, making it more dangerous the longer a match lasts. Top players prioritize the Reaper for leaderboard pushes because its AOE coverage ensures that no trash mobs leak through.

### Bear Tamer
If you prefer a mix of safety and destruction, the Bear Tamer is your go-to summon. By calling forth bear phantoms, this hero can nuke enemy packs from a safe distance while the summons themselves act as temporary roadblocks. This "tank and spank" methodology makes the Bear Tamer incredibly versatile for solo players who need to cover multiple roles at once.

### Demon Knight
The Demon Knight is a technical powerhouse. It relies on combo-based DPS that rewards smart placement and timing. When paired with other demon-type buffs, the Demon Knight can shatter boss health bars in seconds. It is widely regarded as the pinnacle of combo power in the current **summon hero tier** meta.

> **💡 Tip:** S-tier heroes are rare. If you manage to roll any of these, stop rerolling and focus all your resources on leveling them immediately.

## A-Tier: Reliable High-Performance Anchors

A-tier heroes are the backbone of most successful rosters. While they might not have the "infinite scaling" of an S-tier unit, they provide consistent, high-level performance that can carry you through almost any standard map or mid-level leaderboard run.

| Hero | Key Strength | Best Placement |
|---|---|---|
| **Laser Cyborg** | Infinite Piercing Beams | Straight Corridors |
| **Captain Dreadmore** | Cannon Support Trails | Mid-Map Junctions |
| **Mermaid** | Charm/Crowd Control | Near Lane Entrances |
| **Diver** | High Burst Mobility | Boss Spawn Points |

### Laser Cyborg and Captain Dreadmore
The Laser Cyborg is a favorite for players who enjoy futuristic aesthetics and high-precision gameplay. Its beam attacks can pierce through multiple enemies, making it devastating on maps with long, straight paths. Meanwhile, Captain Dreadmore offers a unique naval-style defense. His cannon support provides consistent AOE damage that helps soften up tanky waves before they reach your primary defenses.

### Mermaid and Diver
The Mermaid excels in utility, using her charm ability to temporarily turn enemies against each other or stall them in place. This is vital for managing high-speed waves that might otherwise overwhelm your DPS. The Diver, conversely, is built for aggression. Its ability to "dive" into packs allows it to deal massive burst damage exactly where it is needed most.

## B-Tier: Niche and Situational Units

The B-tier represents heroes that are "fine" but not "great." They are often used as fillers or for very specific elemental strategies. If you are a new player, these units will help you clear the first few worlds, but you should look to replace them as soon as you have enough gems for more high-level summons.

*   **Ice Mage:** Excellent for slowing down bosses, but lacks the raw damage to finish them off.
*   **Fire Mage:** Provides decent Damage Over Time (DOT), though many late-game enemies have high fire resistance.
*   **Archer:** A solid ranged starter, but the lack of AOE means it gets easily overwhelmed by swarms.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not over-invest in B-tier units. Leveling them past level 40 is often a waste of resources that could be spent on A or S-tier heroes.

## C-Tier: The Reroll Fodder

In any **summon hero tier** list, there are units that simply cannot keep up with the power creep of the game. C-tier heroes like the **Swordsman** and **Bandit Nick** are essentially "starter traps." 

The Swordsman offers basic melee swings with no range or sustain, making him useless against any enemy that isn't a basic grunt. Bandit Nick is even more frustrating; while he teases gold-stealing mechanics, the amount of gold gained is negligible compared to the damage loss you suffer by having him on the field. If your first summon is a C-tier unit, it is highly recommended to restart your roll or use your gems to try again.

## Advanced Summoning Strategies

To truly master the **summon hero tier** system, you must understand how to manage your resources. Summoning is not just about luck; it's about maximizing your opportunities.

1.  **Save for Pity:** Many banners in [Roblox](https://www.roblox.com/) games feature a "pity" mechanic. Ensure you have enough gems to hit the guaranteed legendary mark before spending.
2.  **Synergy Over Raw Power:** An A-tier unit with the right buffs (like Seraph's holy buffs) can sometimes outperform an S-tier unit that has no support.
3.  **Map Awareness:** Some heroes perform better on specific terrain. For example, the Laser Cyborg is much stronger on maps with linear paths than on winding, circular maps.

## FAQ

**Q: Which hero is the best for farming gems in 2026?**

A: The **Reaper** is currently considered the best for gem farming due to its massive AOE and fast clear times. If you don't have a Reaper, the **Bear Tamer** is a close second.

**Q: Should I keep an A-tier hero or keep rolling for an S-tier?**

A: If you are a beginner, keep the A-tier hero. It will provide enough power to help you farm the gems needed for future rolls. Only veteran players with established teams should "chase" S-tier units exclusively.

**Q: How often does the summon hero tier list change?**

A: The meta usually shifts with major game updates, which occur every 2-3 months. Always check for balance patches that might buff lower-tier units like the Fire Mage or Ice Mage.

**Q: Is Demon Knight better than Divine?**

A: They serve different roles. Demon Knight is a primary damage dealer, while Divine is a top-tier support. In a perfect team, you would actually want to run both together for maximum efficiency.
