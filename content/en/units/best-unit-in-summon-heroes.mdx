## Understanding Rarity vs. Potential

In Summon Heroes, the base rarity of a unit (Epic, Legendary, Mythic) determines their initial kit and stat ceiling. However, the "Star Level" system allows lower-rarity units to bridge the gap. An Epic unit raised to a Mythic star level can often outperform a base Legendary unit that hasn't been upgraded.

When searching for the **best unit in summon heroes**, you should look for characters with "consistent kits"—meaning their abilities provide value regardless of the enemy's level. Summoners and high-frequency attackers currently dominate the leaderboards because they provide meat shields for the team or bypass enemy defenses through sheer volume of attacks.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Do not ignore your Epic units. Many of the top 10 units for clearing end-game content are actually Epics that have been promoted to Mythic star levels.

### Demon Hunter
The Demon Hunter is frequently cited as the **best unit in summon heroes** for raw damage. He excels at the "range game," allowing you to kite enemies while dealing massive damage from a safe distance.

While she might look unassuming, the Mermaid is one of the most consistent damage dealers in the game. Her basic attack summons a school of seven fish that lock onto enemies. This attack speed is arguably more consistent than higher-rarity units like the Jester, making her a staple for sustained combat.

## Best Tanks and Crowd Control

Surviving the end-game requires more than just high damage; you need units that can soak up hits and control the battlefield. These units are essential for protecting your squishy damage dealers.

| Unit Name | Role | Special Effect | Survival Rating |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dragoon** | Speed Tank | Piercing Energy Spears | ★★★★★ |
| **Diver** | AoE Tank | Heavy Splash Damage | ★★★★☆ |
| **Wind Samurai** | Crowd Control | Tornado Displacement | ★★★☆☆ |
| **Cyber DJ** | Utility Tank | Laser Dance Party | ★★★★☆ |

### Dragoon
The Dragoon is often preferred over other tanks due to her incredible speed and homing abilities. Her "Piercing Energy Spears" chase down enemies, ensuring that she maintains aggro even against mobile bosses. She is widely seen in high-level PvP and mythic-tier PvE content.

### Wind Samurai
Inspired by classic MOBA mechanics, the Wind Samurai uses tornadoes to clear entire paths of enemies. His ability deals roughly 400% of his base damage and provides essential crowd control in the Sakura area stages. While he isn't as tanky as the Diver, his ability to negate enemy movement makes him a top-tier tactical choice.

## The Power of Summoners

Summoners are currently "broken" in the best way possible. By summoning additional entities, these units effectively increase your team size, forcing AI enemies to target disposable minions instead of your main heroes.

1.  **Slime Summoner:** She launches homing slime balls and summons three melee slimes. These slimes are fast and act as excellent distractions for bosses.
2.  **Bear Tamer:** An honorable mention that many veterans swear by. The Bear Tamer summons cubs and a massive bear to fight. In the end-game, these summons scale exceptionally well, especially when paired with buffs from units like the Mythic Reaper.

> **Warning:** When running a summoner-heavy team, be mindful of AoE (Area of Effect) attacks from bosses, which can wipe out your minions instantly if they are clustered together.

## How to Build Your Team

To find the **best unit in summon heroes** for your specific account, you must look at your current roster's synergy. A team of five glass cannons will fail just as quickly as a team of five tanks.

### The Ideal Team Composition
*   **1 Main Tank:** (Dragoon or Diver) to hold the front line.
*   **1 Crowd Control:** (Wind Samurai or Cyber DJ) to manage mob density.
*   **2 Primary DPS:** (Demon Hunter and Mermaid) for boss killing.
*   **1 Flex Unit:** (Slime Summoner or Spellblade) depending on the stage requirements.

## Progression Strategy for 2026

As you progress through the 2026 campaign, focus your resources on one unit at a time. Spreading your upgrade materials across ten different characters will leave you with a mediocre team.

1.  **Identify your Carry:** Choose one DPS unit (like Demon Hunter) and pour your resources into reaching their Mythic star level first.
2.  **Upgrade your Tank:** Once your DPS can clear waves, ensure your tank can survive at least three hits from a stage boss.
3.  **Focus on "Easier" Units:** Units like the Bear Tamer and Slime Summoner are generally easier to obtain and upgrade than Legendaries, making them more efficient for F2P (Free to Play) players.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the absolute best unit in summon heroes for beginners?**

A: The Demon Hunter is widely considered the best for beginners because of his long-range safety and high burst damage. He is easy to play and remains relevant all the way through the end-game.

**Q: Are Legendary units always better than Epics?**

A: Not necessarily. While Legendaries have "crazier kits," a Mythic-star Epic unit often has better stats and more consistent performance than a base-level Legendary. Units like Frame Rate have been known to struggle with accuracy, whereas Epics like the Mermaid are incredibly reliable.

**Q: Why are summoners considered "broken" in the current meta?**

A: Summoners are powerful because their minions soak up damage that would otherwise hit your heroes. In high-level content, the AI tends to target the closest unit, which is almost always a summoned slime or bear, giving your main units free reign to attack.

**Q: Is Cyber DJ worth building for the end-game?**

A: Yes, Cyber DJ is a very tanky unit with solid stats. Her "Laser Dance Party" ability provides excellent frontal damage, though some players find her a bit niche compared to the Dragoon's mobility.
