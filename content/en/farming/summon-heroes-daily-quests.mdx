## The Daily Core: Nightmare Circus and AFK Rewards

The backbone of your daily progression in Summon Heroes lies in two primary activities: the Nightmare Circus and the Dojo's AFK mode. These are non-negotiable tasks if you want to keep your Gem count high and your heroes leveled.

### Mastering the Nightmare Circus
The Nightmare Circus is an endless wave mode that tests the endurance and synergy of your team. It is a daily activity that can be completed a total of three times per day. The further you progress through the waves, the higher the rewards.

| Wave Milestone | Gem Reward (Approx.) | Time Investment |
|---|---|---|
| **Wave 50** | 100 Gems | 2 Minutes |
| **Wave 100** | 220 Gems | 3.5 Minutes |
| **Wave 130+** | 300+ Gems | 4.5 Minutes |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't just "set and forget" the Circus. Manual activation of AoE ultimates during boss waves (every 10 levels) can push you significantly further than auto-battle alone.

### Passive Income: The Dojo AFK Mode
If you find yourself short on time to complete every single quest, the AFK mode in the main dojo is your best friend. You passively gain rewards the longer you remain in this mode. On average, players can expect to earn around **500 Gems per hour** simply by letting the game run or idling. This is also the most consistent way to farm "food" (rice cakes) used to level up your units.

## Understanding the Summoning System and Pity

Summoning is the heart of the game, and doing it efficiently is a core part of the **summon heroes daily quests** philosophy. Banners in Summon Heroes are unique because they rotate every 30 minutes, offering different featured units.

### Banner Composition and Odds
Each summon pack contains a specific distribution of rarities. Knowing these odds helps you decide when to spend your hard-earned 100 Gems (or 1,000 Gems for a 10-pull).

| Rarity | Number of Units on Banner | Drop Chance |
|---|---|---|
| **Mythic** | 1 | 0.1% |
| **Legendary** | 1 | Guaranteed via Pity |
| **Epic** | 2 | Moderate |
| **Rare** | 3 | High |

The game features a **legendary pity system** that guarantees a legendary unit within 100 summons. However, a critical detail often missed by new players is that the pity system is **not shared** between different banners. If you start pulling on one pack, try to reach your goal before the rotation changes, or be prepared to start your count fresh on a new banner.

## The 5-Minute Shop Strategy

One of the most overlooked aspects of the **summon heroes daily quests** routine is the Item Shop. Unlike many games where shops reset once a day, the Summon Heroes hub shop resets every **5 minutes**.

Frequent check-ins allow you to stock up on:
1.  **Summon Tickets:** Occasionally appear for gold or discounted gems.
2.  **Rice Cakes:** Essential for bubbling up (leveling) your units quickly.
3.  **Fusion Crystals:** The primary currency used to increase the star rarity of your heroes.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid using the "Auto-Sell" feature for Rare and Epic units. While it keeps your inventory clean, these units are vital for the fusion system.

## Unit Fusion: Building Five-Star Heroes

Progression in 2026 relies heavily on the fusion system. Unlike other gacha games that require multiple copies of the exact same hero, Summon Heroes allows you to fuse any units of the same star rarity.

### How Fusion Works
To upgrade a four-star unit (like a Dragoon) to a five-star unit, you simply need to sacrifice other four-star units you currently own. This makes every single pull valuable, regardless of whether you like the specific hero or not.

**Fusion Requirements Table:**
| Target Rarity | Required Base Level | Sacrifice Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **4-Star to 5-Star** | Max Level | Any 3x 4-Star Units |
| **5-Star to 6-Star** | Max Level | Any 4x 5-Star Units |
| **Legendary Tier** | Max Level | Specific Fusion Crystals |

By checking the [Official Summon Heroes Community Hub](https://www.pocketgamer.com/) for the latest tier lists, you can identify which "fodder" units are safe to sacrifice and which should be kept for your main team.

## Best Early-Game Units to Target

While legendary and mythic units are the ultimate goal, your **summon heroes daily quests** will be much easier if you build a solid foundation with high-tier Rare units.

*   **Bear Tamer (Rare):** Currently widely considered one of the best units in the game due to his high survivability and ease of acquisition. If you see him on a rotating banner, prioritize your pulls there.
*   **Mermaid (Rare):** Exceptional for the Nightmare Circus. Her AoE (Area of Effect) damage allows her to clear waves of enemies simultaneously, speeding up your Gem farming.

A common "pro" starting team consists of two Mermaids for wave clear and one Bear Tamer to act as a frontline tank.

## Maximizing Daily Efficiency

To get the most out of your playtime in 2026, follow this optimized checklist:

1.  **Login and Collect AFK Rewards:** Do this first to clear your inventory space.
2.  **Check the Shop:** Buy any Fusion Crystals or Rice Cakes available.
3.  **Run Nightmare Circus (3/3):** Aim for at least Wave 100 to maximize Gem income.
4.  **Perform Daily Summons:** Check the banner rotation. If a Bear Tamer or a desired Legendary is up, do your 10-pulls.
5.  **Fuse and Level:** Use the crystals and food gathered to push your main team's star rating higher.
6.  **Return to AFK Mode:** If you aren't actively playing, leave your character in the Dojo.

## FAQ

**Q: How often do the summon banners change in Summon Heroes?**

A: The banners rotate every 30 minutes. Each rotation features a different set of one legendary, two epics, and three rare units. Always check the timer before starting a summoning session to ensure you don't lose track of your specific banner's pity.

**Q: What is the fastest way to complete summon heroes daily quests for Gems?**

A: The fastest method is a combination of the Nightmare Circus (which yields ~300 Gems in 4 minutes) and the AFK Dojo mode (which gives ~500 Gems per hour). Completing your three daily Circus runs should be your top priority.

**Q: Should I sell my low-rarity heroes for extra gold?**

A: Generally, no. It is highly recommended to keep your Rare and Epic units for the Fusion system. Fusing them into your stronger units to increase their star rarity provides much more value in the long run than the small amount of gold gained from selling them.

**Q: Does the pity count carry over to the next banner?**

A: No. Each 30-minute banner has its own separate pity system. If you are close to the 100-pull legendary pity, you must complete those pulls on that specific banner before it rotates, or the progress will be lost.
