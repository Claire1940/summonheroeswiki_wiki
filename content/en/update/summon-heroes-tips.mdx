## Mastering the Summoning Banners

The summoning system is the heartbeat of your progression. Unlike many other gacha-style games, Summon Heroes utilizes a rotating banner system that changes every 30 minutes. This means if you don't see a unit you want, you only have a short wait before a fresh set of heroes becomes available. Each banner typically features seven different units with varying pull rates.

### Banner Rarity and Odds

When approaching the summon area, you will find two different packs. Understanding the distribution of units is key to managing your expectations and your gem wallet.

| Rarity | Number of Units | Pull Chance |
|---|---|---|
| **Mythic** | 1 | 0.1% |
| **Legendary** | 1 | Pity at 100 |
| **Epic** | 2 | ~14.5% |
| **Rare** | 3 | Common |

One of the most important **summon heroes tips** regarding banners is the pity system. The game guarantees a legendary unit within 100 summons. However, a critical detail often missed by new players is that **pity is not shared between banners**. If you have done 50 pulls on Banner A, those do not count toward the pity threshold of Banner B. Always check which banner you are investing in to ensure you hit that 100-pull milestone effectively.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not spread your gems across multiple banners simultaneously. Focus on one until you hit the legendary pity or obtain your desired unit to avoid wasting your progress.

## Efficient Unit Fusion and Growth

A common mistake in Summon Heroes is using the "Auto Sell" feature for Rare and Epic units. While it might seem like an easy way to clear inventory, it actively hinders your long-term power. The fusion system in this game is remarkably flexible, allowing you to upgrade units without needing exact duplicates.

### How Fusion Works

To increase a unit's star rarity, you simply need other units of the same star level. For example, if you want to turn a 4-star Dragoon into a 5-star powerhouse, you can sacrifice any other 4-star units you currently own. This makes every pull valuable, regardless of whether the unit itself is "meta."

| Current Star Level | Required Sacrifice | Resulting Star Level |
|---|---|---|
| **1-Star** | Any 1-Star Unit | 2-Star |
| **2-Star** | Any 2-Star Unit | 3-Star |
| **3-Star** | Any 3-Star Unit | 4-Star |
| **4-Star** | Any 4-Star Unit | 5-Star |

To facilitate this growth, you will need Fusion Crystals. These can be obtained through gameplay or purchased directly from the item shop.

## Farming Gems and Resources

Gems are the primary currency for summoning, and luckily, the game provides several avenues to earn them. If you are looking for **summon heroes tips** on wealth accumulation, focus on the following three methods:

### 1. The Nightmare Circus
This is a daily activity that can be performed three times per day. It functions as an endless wave mode where the difficulty ramps up as you progress. The rewards scale with your performance; reaching level 130 can net you upwards of 300 gems in just a few minutes. 

### 2. AFK Mode (The Dojo)
For players who cannot be active 24/7, the AFK mode in the main dojo is a lifesaver. By simply leaving your character in this zone, you passively accumulate rewards.
*   **Gem Rate:** Approximately 500 gems per hour.
*   **Bonus Loot:** You will also receive significant amounts of gold and food (like rice cakes) to level up your units.

### 3. The Item Shop
Located in the main hub, the item shop is a goldmine for savvy players. It resets every 5 minutes. You should check it frequently to buy:
*   **Summon Tickets:** Often cheaper than direct gem pulls.
*   **Rice Cakes:** Essential for leveling up units quickly.
*   **Fusion Crystals:** Necessary for the star-upgrade process.

## Best Early Game Units to Target

Not all heroes are created equal, especially when you are just starting your journey. While Legendaries are the end goal, certain Rare and Epic units punch far above their weight class.

*   **Bear Tamer (Rare):** Widely considered one of the best units in the game despite his lower rarity. He is easy to obtain and provides massive utility.
*   **Mermaid (Rare):** An exceptional choice for early-game wave clearing. Her AoE (Area of Effect) damage allows her to wipe out groups of slimes and bandits simultaneously.
*   **Dual Wielder (Epic):** A fantastic front-line tank. He can soak up significant damage while your ranged units like Archers or Mages deal damage from the backline.

> **💡 Tip:** A starter team consisting of one Bear Tamer and two Mermaids is a highly effective "budget" build that can clear most of the initial stages with ease.

## Combat Strategy and Progression

When you enter a stage, your units will automatically move toward the enemy. However, your role is to manage their upgrades and ensure they are positioned correctly.

1.  **Level Up During Battle:** As you defeat enemies, you gain experience. Ensure you are clicking the level-up prompts for your active units to keep their HP and damage high enough for the boss encounter.
2.  **Focus on the Tank:** In stages like the Bandit camp, your melee units (like the Dual Wielder) will take the brunt of the damage. If their health gets low, consider swapping them or prioritizing their level-ups to increase their survivability.
3.  **Utilize Auto-Abilities:** If the game features an "Auto" toggle for abilities, keep it on during farm sessions, but consider manual control for difficult boss fights to time your high-damage bursts.

## FAQ

**Q: How often do the summon banners change?**

A: The banners in Summon Heroes rotate every 30 minutes, offering a new selection of units and different legendary pity tracks.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get gems?**

A: The fastest active way is completing the Nightmare Circus three times daily. For passive income, leaving your character in AFK mode in the dojo provides about 500 gems per hour.

**Q: Should I sell my Rare units for extra gold?**

A: No! One of the most important **summon heroes tips** is to keep your Rare and Epic units for fusion. You can use them as "fodder" to upgrade your stronger units to higher star levels.

**Q: Does the pity system carry over between different banners?**

A: No, each banner has its own independent pity counter. You must perform 100 summons on a specific banner to guarantee a legendary from that specific set.
