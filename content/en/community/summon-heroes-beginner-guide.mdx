## Understanding the Summoning System

The heart of the game lies in the Summon Area. Unlike many other gacha games where banners last for weeks, Summon Heroes features a high-speed rotation system. There are typically two different packs available at any given time, and these rotate every 30 minutes. This means if you don't see a unit you like, you only have to wait a short while for a fresh set of heroes to appear.

Each banner contains a total of seven different units with varying pull rates. Understanding these odds is the first step in managing your expectations and your gem economy.

| Rarity | Number of Units | Pull Chance |
|---|---|---|
| **Mythic** | 1 | **0.1%** |
| **Legendary** | 1 | Guaranteed via Pity |
| **Epic** | 2 | ~14.5% |
| **Rare** | 3 | High |

One of the most player-friendly features is the legendary pity system. You are guaranteed a legendary unit within 100 summons. However, there is a major caveat: **pity is not shared between banners.** If you spend 50 pulls on Banner A and then switch to Banner B, your progress toward a guaranteed legendary on Banner A remains there, but does not help you on Banner B. Always commit to one banner until you hit your target or the pity threshold.

## The Power of Unit Fusion

A common mistake highlighted in any **summon heroes beginner guide** is the tendency to auto-sell lower-rarity units. While the game provides an "Auto Sell" toggle for Rare and Epic units, you should almost always leave this turned off. 

Summon Heroes uses a unique fusion system that allows you to upgrade the star rarity of your favorite units using fodder of the same star level. For example, if you have a four-star Dragoon, you do not need another Dragoon to upgrade it. You can take any other four-star unit you own and fuse it into the Dragoon to transform it into a five-star powerhouse.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Never sell your Rare or Epic units early on. They are the essential "fuel" required to evolve your Legendary and Mythic units to higher star levels.

To facilitate this fusion, you will need Fusion Crystals. These can be obtained through gameplay or purchased directly from the Item Shop in the main hub. The shop is a goldmine for progression, resetting every 5 minutes with items like:
*   **Summon Tickets:** For extra pulls without spending gems.
*   **Rice Cakes:** Food items used to level up your units quickly.
*   **Fusion Crystals:** Various rarities to match your unit's current star level.

## Currency Farming and Resource Management

To keep summoning, you need a steady stream of gems. For beginners, the game offers several avenues to accumulate wealth without spending real money. Gems are the primary currency used for summons (100 gems per single pull, 1,000 for a 10-pull).

| Activity | Rewards | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Main Story Stages** | Gems, Coins, Candy | One-time per stage |
| **Nightmare Circus** | High Gem yield | 3 Times Daily |
| **AFK Mode (Dojo)** | ~500 Gems per Hour | Continuous |
| **Item Shop** | Discounted Tickets | Every 5 Minutes |

The **Nightmare Circus** is an endless wave mode that tests the endurance of your team. The further you progress, the higher the gem payout. A solid 4-minute run can net you upwards of 300 gems, making it the most efficient active farming method.

If you are busy or need to step away from the keyboard, the **AFK Mode** in the main dojo is your best friend. In 2026, the rates remain generous, providing roughly 500 gems every hour along with a significant amount of unit food and gold. Always leave your character in the dojo before logging off for the night.

## Best Early Game Units to Target

Not all units are created equal, and some low-rarity heroes punch significantly above their weight class. When looking at the rotating banners, keep an eye out for these specific characters to jumpstart your progress.

### 1. Bear Tamer (Rare)
Despite being a Rare unit, the Bear Tamer is widely considered one of the best units for the early and mid-game. He is incredibly easy to obtain and provides consistent damage and utility that rivals some Epic units. Because he is easy to pull, you can quickly find duplicates or fodder to raise his star level.

### 2. Mermaid (Rare)
The Mermaid is the queen of Area of Effect (AoE) damage. In modes like the Nightmare Circus or stages with high enemy density (like the Slime Summoner tutorial boss), her ability to clear entire waves at once is invaluable. A common starter strategy involves running two Mermaids alongside a tankier unit.

### 3. Dual Wielder (Epic)
If you manage to pull the Dual Wielder, you have found your primary tank. With high HP and the ability to stay on the front lines, he protects your squishier mages and archers. He is a 14.5% drop on many banners, making him a realistic target for players who have finished the initial tutorial.

## Combat Basics and Team Composition

Combat in Summon Heroes is largely automated, but your input matters in team placement and unit selection. Your team typically consists of three active heroes. A balanced team usually follows the "Tank, DPS, Support/AoE" triangle.

When starting Stage 1 against the Bandits, ensure your highest HP unit (like the Dual Wielder or a high-star Dragoon) is in the lead. Your archers and mages should be positioned to deal damage from the backline. While the game features an "Auto" button for abilities, manually timing your ultimate skills can sometimes be the difference between victory and defeat against high-HP bosses.

## Advanced Tips for Success

*   **Check the Shop Constantly:** Because the shop resets every 5 minutes, you can stockpile Fusion Crystals and Rice Cakes very quickly. This is the fastest way to "power-level" a new unit you just pulled.
*   **Don't Ignore the Tutorial:** Completing the initial Slime Summoner fight and the Bandit stage provides the base currency needed for your first "real" summons.
*   **Equip Best Feature:** Use the "Equip Best" button in the unit menu frequently. As you pull new Epics and Legendaries, the game will automatically calculate the highest stat combinations for your active team.
*   **Star Rarity > Base Rarity:** A 5-star Rare unit is often stronger than a 4-star Legendary. Don't be afraid to use highly evolved Rares until you have the resources to fuse your Legendaries.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the pity count reset when the banner rotates?**

A: No, the pity count is tied to that specific banner slot. However, since banners rotate every 30 minutes, it is best to check if the pity is "shared" between the two active slots. Generally, each of the two banner circles maintains its own independent pity counter.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get gems in this summon heroes beginner guide?**

A: The fastest active way is the Nightmare Circus, which you can run three times a day. For passive income, staying in the AFK Dojo is the most reliable method, yielding about 500 gems per hour.

**Q: Should I use gems to buy items in the shop?**

A: Generally, no. Save your gems for summoning units. The gold (coins) you earn from AFK farming and stages should be used for shop items. Only spend gems in the shop if there is a highly discounted Summon Ticket or a rare Fusion Crystal you desperately need.

**Q: Can I play Summon Heroes solo?**

A: Yes, the majority of the game is designed for solo progression through stages and the Nightmare Circus. However, engaging with the community can help you discover which banners are currently "hot" or which units have been buffed in recent 2026 updates.
