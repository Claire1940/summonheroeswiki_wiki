## Understanding the Grading System

Before diving into the specific rankings, it is essential to understand how units are categorized. In Summon Heroes, units are evaluated based on their role, rarity, and specific performance metrics like attack speed and cooldown.

The grading system we utilize is as follows:
*   **X-Tier (Core):** These are the absolute best units in the game. They are essential for end-game content and provide unparalleled value.
*   **S-Tier (Elite):** Highly powerful units that excel in almost every situation. They are usually the primary carries for most players.
*   **A-Tier (Great):** Strong units that are reliable but may have slight niche limitations or are slightly outclassed by S-tier alternatives.
*   **B-Tier (Situational):** Average units that are useful in the early to mid-game but should be replaced as you progress.
*   **F-Tier (Avoid):** Units that suffer from poor scaling, long animation locks, or low DPS.

## Summon Hero Tierlist: The Top Tier Units (X & S Rank)

The units in these tiers represent the pinnacle of power in Summon Heroes. If you manage to pull one of these, they should immediately become the focal point of your team building and resource investment.

### Divine (X-Tier)
Divine is widely considered one of the best ranged units currently available. Her specialty lies in her ability usage, which allows her to inflict massive bursts of damage that can clear entire waves of enemies. Unlike other ranged units that rely on consistent but lower damage, Divine's kit is designed for high-impact moments.

### BET (X-Tier)
Every team needs a backbone, and BET serves as the ultimate tank. With the highest health pool in the game as of April 2026, BET is indispensable for story progression and difficult boss encounters. He doesn't just sit there and take hits, however; his DPS is surprisingly high for a tank, making him a dual-threat unit.

### Seraph & Matriarch (S-Tier)
These units provide incredible utility and damage. Seraph is often favored for her consistency in both PvE and PvP environments. Matriarch, on the other hand, offers unique synergies that can elevate the performance of your entire squad, making her a "force multiplier" in high-level play.

> **💡 Tip:** When using BET, ensure he is placed at the front of your formation to maximize his health pool, allowing your glass-cannon DPS units like Divine to fire safely from the backline.

## Legendary Spotlight: Ranger and Sage

Legendary units are the goal for most players, but not all Legendaries are created equal. In the current **summon hero tierlist**, two units stand out for their specific mechanics.

### The Ranger: The Boss Killer
The Ranger is a revolutionary unit because he was the first to introduce the Damage Over Time (DOT) mechanic to the game. 
*   **DOT Mechanic:** He burns down units rapidly, making him the premier choice for boss fights where health pools are massive.
*   **Homing Attacks:** His projectiles track enemies, ensuring that his DPS remains consistent even against fast-moving targets.
*   **Range:** He boasts impressive reach, allowing him to stay out of the "danger zone" of most melee-centric bosses.

### The Sage: The Ranged Specialist
While the Sage is ranked slightly lower than the Ranger in terms of raw boss-killing potential (A-Tier), she remains highly relevant. Her ability makes her a formidable ranged DPS unit, and she performs exceptionally well when enemies get within mid-range. She is a great secondary DPS to pair with a primary carry.

## Epic Units: The Best Mid-Game Options

You won't always have a full roster of Mythicals and Legendaries. Knowing which Epic units to keep can bridge the gap during your progression. The two standout units in this category are the **Thief** and the **Construct**.

| Unit | Role | Why They Are Good |
|---|---|---|
| **Thief** | Melee DPS | Fast attack speed and great DPS for both PvE and PvP. |
| **Construct** | Melee Bruiser | Very bulky and capable of tanking significant damage in early game. |

The Thief is particularly notable for its fast attack animations, which allow it to output damage more reliably than some slower Legendary units. If you are struggling with a specific level, swapping in a high-level Thief can often provide the speed needed to clear mobs before they overwhelm your tank.

## Units to Avoid: The "Trap" Picks

One of the most important aspects of a **summon hero tierlist** is identifying which units are not worth your resources. In Summon Heroes, high rarity does not always equate to high performance.

### Frame Rate (Legendary)
Despite being a Legendary unit, Frame Rate is currently ranked in the B-Tier. The primary issue is "animation lock." His attack animations are so slow that he often gets hit or killed before completing his move. Furthermore, he has a tendency to rush into dangerous situations only to die immediately, leading to a net loss in team efficiency.

### Academy Witch (Common/Rare)
The Academy Witch is widely considered one of the worst units in the entire game. Her damage is negligible, and she lacks the utility needed to justify a slot on any team. If you are starting out, use her only until you find literally any other unit, then replace her immediately.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not invest heavily in "Frame Rate" just because he is a Legendary. His current AI and animation speeds make him a liability in competitive play.

## How to Optimize Your Team Composition

Building a team in Summon Heroes isn't just about picking the five highest-ranked units on the **summon hero tierlist**. You must balance roles to ensure your team can survive various challenges.

1.  **The Anchor (1 Tank):** Units like **BET** or the **Construct** should always be present to soak up damage.
2.  **The Primary Carry (1-2 DPS):** Focus on high-damage units like **Divine** or **Seraph**.
3.  **The Specialist (1 Utility/DOT):** Use the **Ranger** for boss-heavy stages to utilize his burn damage.
4.  **The Flex Slot (1 Support/DPS):** Depending on the stage, add another ranged unit like **Sage** or a fast melee unit like **Thief**.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most important stat in the summon hero tierlist?**

A: While attack power is important, **DPS (Damage Per Second)** and **Ability Cooldown** are often more critical. A unit with high attack but a very long cooldown or slow animation (like Frame Rate) will often be outclassed by a faster unit with lower base attack.

**Q: Is the Ranger better than Divine for story mode?**

A: It depends on the stage. For stages with a single, high-HP boss, the Ranger’s DOT (Damage Over Time) is superior. For stages with many small mobs, Divine’s ability-based area damage is generally more effective at clearing the screen.

**Q: Should I keep Epic units once I have Legendaries?**

A: Yes, specifically the **Thief** and **Construct**. Because Epics are easier to upgrade and evolve, a maxed-out Thief can sometimes outperform a base-level Legendary that you haven't had the resources to improve yet.

**Q: How do I check the specific DPS of my units?**

A: You can access the "Unit Data" section within the game's Unit Index. This provides a detailed breakdown of attack speed, base damage, and how their abilities scale with levels, which is essential for verifying their placement on any **summon hero tierlist**.
