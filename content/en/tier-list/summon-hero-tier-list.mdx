## X-Tier: The Meta-Defining Units

The units in this category are the gold standard for 2026. If you manage to summon any of these, they should immediately become the focal point of your team composition.

### Divine
Divine remains one of the most sought-after units in Summon Heroes. As a ranged specialist, Divine focuses on massive ability usage to inflict devastating burst damage. In both PvE and PvP, Divine can clear waves of enemies before they even reach your front line.

### BET
When it comes to survivability, BET is unrivaled. Boasting the highest health in the game, BET serves as the ultimate tank for story progression. Unlike traditional tanks that sacrifice offense for defense, BET maintains a surprisingly high DPS, making it a "juggernaut" that can soak up damage and dish it back out.

### Ranger
The Ranger is a unique addition to the top tier because it was the first unit to introduce Damage Over Time (DOT) mechanics. This makes the Ranger an essential boss killer. With homing projectiles and impressive range, the Ranger can burn down high-HP targets safely from a distance.

## S-Tier: Powerful Alternatives

Units in the S-tier are incredibly potent and are often just a few stat points away from being X-tier. They are excellent for filling gaps in your team or serving as primary carries if you haven't pulled a Core unit yet.

### Sage
Sage is a legendary ranged DPS unit that remains highly relevant in 2026 due to her ability kit. While her base damage is slightly lower than Divine's, her ability allows her to maintain high pressure even when enemies close the gap. She is a fantastic choice for players who need consistent, reliable damage.

### Construct (Epic)
The Construct is proof that rarity isn't everything. As an Epic unit, it is significantly easier to obtain than Legendaries, yet it functions as a top-tier melee bruiser. It is exceptionally bulky and capable of tanking significant hits while maintaining a steady stream of damage.

### Thief (Epic)
The Thief excels in fast-paced encounters. With a high attack speed and respectable DPS, this melee unit is a favorite for PvP players who want to overwhelm opponents quickly. Its niche application in certain PvE stages also keeps it high on the **summon hero tier list**.

## A-Tier and B-Tier: Niche and Mid-Game Units

As you progress through Summon Heroes, you will likely spend a lot of time using A-tier units. These are solid performers that get the job done but may struggle against the most difficult endgame bosses.

*   **Sage (Standard Rank):** While her peak performance is S-tier, standard builds often land in A-tier.
*   **Ice Mage:** The quintessential starter unit. While great for learning the game, the Ice Mage lacks the scaling necessary for late-game content.
*   **Frame Rate (Legendary):** A cautionary tale for all players. Despite being a Legendary unit, Frame Rate is often ranked in B-tier or lower. He suffers from "animation lock," meaning he stays stationary for too long during attacks, often leading to him rushing into danger just to die without dealing significant damage.

## Understanding Unit Roles

To maximize the effectiveness of your team, you must balance your roster based on roles. A team of five glass-cannon DPS units will likely fail in harder stages because they lack a frontline.

1.  **Academy (Close Range DPS):** High risk, high reward. These units deal massive damage but require protection.
2.  **Archer / Ranged DPS:** Essential for staying safe while chipping away at enemy health. Units like Divine and Ranger fall here.
3.  **Bander / Melee DPS:** The bread and butter of your frontline. They provide consistent pressure.
4.  **Bruiser / Tank:** Units like BET and Construct that exist to soak up damage and protect your squishier heroes.

## Team Composition Strategies

Building a team based on our **summon hero tier list** involves more than just picking the top five units. Synergy is the key to victory in 2026.

*   **The Balanced Build:** One Tank (BET), one DOT specialist (Ranger), and three Ranged DPS (Divine, Seraph, Sage). This setup is ideal for most story missions.
*   **The PvP Rush:** Focusing on high-speed units like the Thief and high-burst units like Divine to end matches before the opponent can set up.
*   **The Boss Killer:** Centering a team around the Ranger's DOT and using multiple tanks/bruisers to keep the Ranger alive as the boss's health drains.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the single best unit in the summon hero tier list?**

A: While "best" can be subjective based on the mode, **Divine** is widely considered the best overall unit due to her massive ability damage and versatility in both PvE and PvP. **BET** is the definitive choice if you need a tank.

**Q: Should I keep Epic units, or only focus on Legendaries?**

A: You should definitely keep high-tier Epics like the **Construct** and **Thief**. In many cases, these units outperform lower-tier Legendaries like Frame Rate because their kits are more optimized for the current 2026 meta.

**Q: Why is Frame Rate considered bad if he is a Legendary unit?**

A: Frame Rate suffers from severe animation lock, which makes his effective DPS much lower than his stats suggest. He often takes damage while stuck in an animation, leading to a quick death in high-level stages.

**Q: How do I check the specific stats of my heroes?**

A: You can access the **Unit Data** section within the game's Unit Index. This provides a full breakdown of HP, Attack, Speed, and Cool Down for every hero you have summoned.
