## The Summon Heroes Ranking System Explained

Before diving into the specific units, it is essential to understand the grading metrics used in this evaluation. Unlike many other gacha-style games where rarity is the only indicator of power, this game features several legendary units that underperform and epic units that can carry a team through difficult stages.

The grading is categorized as follows:
*   **X-Tier (Core):** These are the absolute best units in the game. They define the meta and should be the foundation of any high-level team.
*   **S-Tier (Top Tier):** Highly versatile units that perform exceptionally well in almost every game mode.
*   **A-Tier (Great):** Solid choices that are reliable but may have slight niche requirements or be overshadowed by X-tier alternatives.
*   **B-Tier (Niche):** Units that are usable but often suffer from specific flaws like slow animations or low base stats.
*   **S- / F-Tier (Unusable):** Characters that should be replaced immediately. These units often lack the utility or damage to keep up with even basic story progression.

## X-Tier and S-Tier: The Meta Definitions

The pinnacle of the **tierlist summon heroes** meta is occupied by units that offer unique mechanics, such as Damage over Time (DOT) or massive health pools for tanking. In the current 2026 landscape, units like Matriarch and Divine are considered essential for players looking to clear the hardest dungeons.

### Divine (X-Tier)
Divine is a premier ranged unit that specializes in ability usage to inflict massive burst damage. Unlike standard archers, Divine’s utility comes from her cooldown-based skills that can clear entire waves of enemies. She is widely regarded as one of the best investments for any player due to her consistent DPS and safe positioning.

### BET (X-Tier)
If you need a frontline, BET is the undisputed champion. Boasting the highest health stats currently recorded in the game, BET serves as the ultimate tank for story progression. While his primary role is soaking up damage, his DPS is surprisingly high for a tanking unit, making him a hybrid threat that is difficult to ignore in PvP.

### Ranger (S-Tier)
The Ranger holds a special place in the 2026 meta as the first unit to introduce the Damage over Time (DOT) mechanic. This makes him an incredible "boss killer." His ability allows him to burn down high-health targets rapidly, and his homing projectiles ensure that no damage is wasted, even against mobile enemies.

> **💡 Tip:** When using the Ranger, pair him with units that can slow enemies to ensure his DOT stacks have the maximum amount of time to tick down the enemy's health bar.

### The Value of the Construct
The Construct is a standout in the Epic category. It functions as a "melee bruiser," meaning it has enough health to stay on the frontlines while still dealing respectable damage. For players who haven't yet pulled BET, the Construct is the most reliable alternative for a sturdy frontline.

## Units to Avoid: The Traps of 2026

Not all heroes are created equal. Some units, despite having high rarity or cool designs, suffer from mechanical flaws that make them a liability. The most notable example in the current **tierlist summon heroes** rankings is **Frame Rate**.

Despite being a Legendary unit, Frame Rate is frequently ranked in B-tier or lower. The primary issue is "animation lock." His attack animations take so long to complete that he often stands still while enemies chip away at his health. Furthermore, his AI tendency to "rush in just to die" makes him a frustrating unit to manage in high-stakes PvP.

Another unit to steer clear of is the **Academy Witch**. She is widely considered the worst unit in the game. With abysmal range and low health, she is typically the first to fall in any encounter. If she is currently in your lineup, prioritize replacing her with even a basic Archer or Ice Mage.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not be fooled by rarity alone. A well-built Epic Thief will almost always contribute more to your team's success than a poorly optimized Legendary Frame Rate.

## Building a Balanced Team Composition

To maximize your efficiency in Summon Heroes, you cannot simply stack five DPS units and hope for the best. A balanced team requires a synergy between tanks, ranged attackers, and utility units.

1.  **The Anchor (Tank):** Use **BET** or **Construct**. Their job is to hold the line so your glass cannons don't take damage.
2.  **The Boss Killer (DOT/Single Target):** Use **Ranger**. His ability to melt high-health targets is vital for stage bosses.
3.  **The Wave Clear (AOE):** Use **Divine** or **Seraph**. These units specialize in clearing out the smaller "mobs" that can overwhelm your tank.
4.  **The Flex Spot:** This depends on the content. For PvP, a fast melee unit like **Thief** is excellent for disrupting enemy formations. For PvE, a second ranged unit like **Sage** can provide extra safety.

For more detailed stats on these characters, players should consult the [Summon Heroes Official Index](https://www.roblox.com/games/15316440261/Summon-Heroes) to view real-time data on attack speed and ability cooldowns.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the best overall unit in the current tierlist summon heroes?**

A: While it depends on your team needs, **Divine** and **BET** are generally considered the best. Divine offers unparalleled ranged damage, while BET provides the highest survivability in the game.

**Q: Should I keep Legendary units even if they are ranked low?**

A: It is usually worth keeping one copy of every Legendary for future balance patches. However, in terms of active gameplay, you should not prioritize leveling a low-ranked Legendary like Frame Rate over a high-ranked Epic like Thief.

**Q: How do I find specific stats for my units?**

A: You can access the "Unit Data" section within the game's Unit Index. This provides a deep dive into base attack, DPS, and specific ability mechanics for every hero you have summoned.

**Q: Is the Ranger better than the Sage?**

A: In most scenarios, yes. The Ranger's introduction of the DOT mechanic gives him a significant advantage against bosses, whereas the Sage requires enemies to be closer to reach her maximum DPS potential.
