## X-Tier: The Core Units You Need

The X-tier represents the pinnacle of the **summon heroes tierlist**. These units are considered "core" because they provide utility, tanking, or damage that no other units can replicate effectively. If you manage to pull one of these, they should immediately become the focus of your upgrade resources.

### Divine (Mythical)
Divine is widely regarded as one of the best ranged units in the game. Her specialty lies in ability usage, which allows her to inflict massive bursts of damage from a safe distance. In the current 2026 meta, her DPS output remains unrivaled for clearing high-level story stages and boss encounters.

### BET (Tank/Bruiser)
When it comes to survivability, BET stands alone. Boasting the highest health pool currently available, BET acts as the ultimate sponge for enemy damage. Unlike other tanks that offer little offensive pressure, BET maintains decent DPS, making him a dual-threat unit that ensures your backline stays protected while contributing to the kill count.

### Matriarch and Seraph
Both Matriarch and Seraph are essential for high-level play. Seraph provides incredible utility and consistent damage, while Matriarch is often cited as a top-five unit due to her reliability in various team compositions. These units are the backbone of most successful PvP builds.

## S and A Tier: High-Performance Specialists

While X-tier units are the goal, your team will likely be filled with S and A-tier units for a significant portion of the game. These units are often more accessible but still offer enough power to clear the majority of content.

### Ranger (Legendary)
Ranger is a standout in the **summon heroes tierlist** for being the first unit to introduce Damage Over Time (DOT). This makes him a premier "boss killer." His ability to burn down high-health targets while utilizing homing projectiles and good range makes him a versatile pick for any stage.

### The New Vanguard: Thief and Construct
Two of the newest Epic units have shaken up the rankings. While they are lower in rarity than Legendaries, their kits are surprisingly modern:

*   **Thief:** A melee unit with exceptionally fast attack speed. While slightly niche, his DPS is high enough to rival older Legendaries.
*   **Construct:** A melee bruiser that is very bulky. He is capable of tanking significant damage while dishing out respectable hits, making him an excellent budget alternative to BET.

## Units to Avoid: The Traps of the Meta

One of the most common mistakes players make when consulting a **summon heroes tierlist** is assuming that "Legendary" always equals "Good." Several high-rarity units suffer from mechanical flaws that make them a liability in difficult content.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid investing heavily in "Frame Rate." Despite being a Legendary unit, he suffers from "anim lock" (animation locking). He often rushes into groups of enemies only to die because he cannot move or react during his attack animations.

Furthermore, the **Academy Witch** is currently ranked as one of the worst units in the entire game. Even for beginners, she is often outperformed by the basic **Ice Mage** starter unit. If you have the Academy Witch on your team, replace her as soon as possible with any Epic or higher-tier unit.

## Role Distribution and Team Building

A successful team in Summon Heroes isn't just about picking the five highest-ranked units; it’s about balance. A team of five glass-cannon DPS units will likely fail in high-level PvE because they lack a front line to soak up damage.

When looking at the **summon heroes tierlist**, try to fill these specific roles:

1.  **The Anchor (Tank):** Units like **BET** or **Construct**. Their job is to stand at the front and absorb hits.
2.  **The Boss Killer:** Units with DOT or high single-target burst, such as **Ranger**.
3.  **The Crowd Controller/Ranged DPS:** Units like **Divine** or **Sage** who can clear waves of smaller enemies before they reach your tank.
4.  **The Utility/Support:** Units that provide buffs or have abilities that disrupt enemy movements.

For more detailed stats on specific units, players should always consult the in-game **Unit Data** index. This tool provides granular details on Attack Speed and Ability Cooldowns that can help you fine-tune your team's rotation. ## FAQ

**Q: Who is the best overall unit in the current summon heroes tierlist?**

A: While it depends on the game mode, **Divine** is generally considered the best overall unit due to her massive ranged damage and ability usage. For pure tanking, **BET** is the undisputed champion.

**Q: Is it worth keeping Epic units like Thief and Construct?**

A: Yes. In the 2026 meta, Thief and Construct are high-performing Epics that can often outperform older or poorly optimized Legendary units. They are excellent for players who haven't yet pulled a full team of Mythicals.

**Q: Why is Frame Rate ranked so low if he is a Legendary?**

A: Frame Rate suffers from low base damage and a mechanical issue known as "animation locking." This causes him to stay stationary during attacks, leading to him taking unnecessary damage and dying frequently in high-level stages.

**Q: How often does the summon heroes tierlist change?**

A: The tierlist usually shifts whenever a new update introduces units or adjusts the stats of existing ones. It is recommended to check for updates every few weeks to ensure your team remains optimized for the latest content.
