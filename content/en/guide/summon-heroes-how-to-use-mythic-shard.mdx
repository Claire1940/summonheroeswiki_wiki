## Understanding the Summoning System

Before diving into the specifics of mythic shards, it is vital to understand how the summoning banners function in Summon Heroes. The game features a rotating banner system that updates every 30 minutes. Each pack offers a specific pool of units, making timing essential if you are hunting for a particular hero.

### Banner Composition and Rates

Every summon banner in 2026 follows a strict hierarchy of unit rarities. When you spend your gems, you are pulling from a pool that includes:

| Rarity | Number of Units | Drop Chance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mythic** | 1 | **0.1%** |
| **Legendary** | 1 | **Guaranteed at 100 Pity** |
| **Epic** | 2 | Moderate |
| **Rare** | 3 | High |

Summoning costs 100 gems for a single pull, while a 10-pull requires 1,000 gems. One of the most player-friendly features in Summon Heroes is the legendary pity system. You are guaranteed a legendary unit within 100 summons. However, a crucial tip for all players is that **pity does not carry over between different banners**. If you start pulling on one banner, you should aim to reach the pity threshold before the banner rotates, or you risk losing your progress toward that guaranteed legendary.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Always check the timer on the current banner before committing a large number of gems. If the banner rotates in 5 minutes and you need 50 more pulls for pity, it is better to wait for the next rotation.

## The Fusion System: Upgrading Your Heroes

One of the standout features of Summon Heroes is the flexible fusion system. Unlike other Gacha games that require a duplicate of the exact same hero to upgrade rarity, Summon Heroes allows you to use any unit of the same star level as fodder.

### How Fusion Works

If you have a four-star unit, such as a Dragoon, and you want to upgrade it to a five-star unit, you do not need another Dragoon. You can take any other four-star unit in your inventory and fuse it into your primary hero.

| Current Rarity | Fodder Required | Resulting Rarity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **3-Star** | Any 3-Star Unit | **4-Star** |
| **4-Star** | Any 4-Star Unit | **5-Star** |
| **5-Star** | Specific Fusion Crystals | **6-Star+** |

Because of this mechanic, you should **never auto-sell your Rare or Epic units**. Even if you don't plan on using a specific hero in combat, they serve as essential "food" to level up your main team. You can find more details on unit progression through the [Summon Heroes official community hub](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rZHb4ez5iT8) where players share specific build paths.

> **💡 Tip:** Visit the Item Shop in the main hub every 5 minutes. It frequently stocks Fusion Crystals of various rarities, which are necessary for high-level hero progression.

## Efficient Resource Farming in 2026

To keep up with the demands of summoning and fusing, you need a steady stream of gems and food. There are three primary ways to stay stocked in Summon Heroes.

### 1. Nightmare Circus
The Nightmare Circus is a daily activity that can be performed three times per day. It is an endless wave mode where the rewards scale based on your performance. 
*   **Efficiency:** Reaching level 130 can net you approximately 300 gems in under 5 minutes.
*   **Focus:** Use AoE (Area of Effect) units like the Mermaid to clear waves quickly.

### 2. AFK Mode (Main Dojo)
For passive income, the AFK mode in the Main Dojo is unbeatable. While you are away from the game or simply hanging out in the hub, you passively accumulate rewards.
*   **Gem Rate:** Approximately 500 gems per hour.
*   **Secondary Rewards:** Significant amounts of gold and "rice cakes" (food used to level up units).

### 3. The Item Shop
The shop is the heart of the game's economy. Since it resets every 5 minutes, checking it frequently is mandatory for competitive play. You can purchase summon tickets, fusion crystals, and experience food for gold or gems.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more mythic shards in Summon Heroes?**

A: Mythic shards are primarily obtained through high-level rewards in the Nightmare Circus, special event milestones, and occasionally as rare rotations in the Item Shop. Consistent daily play is the best way to accumulate them.

**Q: Does the legendary pity count toward mythic units?**

A: No. The 100-pull pity system only guarantees a Legendary unit. Mythic units remain a 0.1% drop chance regardless of how many times you have summoned on a specific banner. This is why learning **summon heroes how to use mythic shard** mechanics is so important for guaranteed progression.

**Q: Should I use my gems for summoning or buying items in the shop?**

A: Early on, use gems to build a core team of legendaries through the pity system. Once you have a solid team, shift your gem spending toward Fusion Crystals and Summon Tickets in the Item Shop to maximize your fusion potential.

**Q: Can I fuse a mythic unit with a rare unit?**

A: Yes, as long as they share the same star rarity. For example, if your mythic unit is 5-stars, you can use any other 5-star unit (even a common one that you've upgraded) as fodder to increase the mythic's power.
