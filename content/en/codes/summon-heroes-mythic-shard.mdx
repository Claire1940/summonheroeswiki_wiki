## What Are Mythic Shards?

Mythic Shards are a specialized currency used to craft specific Mythic-tier units. Unlike the standard summoning banners that rely on Gem currency and luck, the shard system is deterministic. For example, the latest powerhouse unit, The Emperor, requires a total of **50 Mythic Shards** to be summoned. 

While you can occasionally find shards through limited-time codes or first-time clear rewards, the bulk of your collection will come from repeatable Dungeons. This shift in game design encourages active play over simple AFK grinding, though both remain viable parts of a healthy account economy.

## Optimizing Your Team for Shard Farming

To farm a **summon heroes mythic shard** effectively, you need a team that maximizes Damage Per Second (DPS) while maintaining enough survivability to withstand boss mechanics. The current meta revolves around specific units that excel in wave clearing and single-target boss damage.

### Top Tier Farming Units
*   **The Reaper:** Exceptional for high-level dungeons due to its scaling damage and ultimate ability.
*   **Jester:** A staple in many high-rank teams. To optimize the Jester, many players disable the "Auto" ability toggle. The Jester's basic melee attack often has higher DPS than its slower skill animations.
*   **Bear Tamer:** Despite being a Rare unit, the Bear Tamer is widely considered one of the best in the game. He is easy to obtain from banners and punches well above his weight class.
*   **Mermaid:** The queen of early-game AoE. If you are just starting your shard journey, two Mermaids can clear early waves quickly, allowing you to focus gems on better units later.

### The Fusion System
Never auto-sell your Rare or Epic units. In Summon Heroes, you can use any unit of the same star rarity as fodder to upgrade your main heroes. For example, any four-star unit can be fused into a four-star Dragoon to elevate it to five stars. This makes every summon valuable, even if you don't plan on using the specific unit in your active party.

## The Economy: Gems vs. Shards

While shards are for crafting specific units, Gems are required for the general summon banners. These banners rotate every 30 minutes, featuring one Legendary, two Epics, and three Rares.

*   **100 Gems:** Single Summon
*   **1,000 Gems:** 10-Pull
*   **Pity System:** Guaranteed Legendary within 100 summons (Note: Pity is *not* shared between different banners).

To sustain your Gem income, utilize the **AFK Mode** in the main dojo. This mode generates approximately 500 Gems per hour, along with food for leveling up units. This is the most efficient way to keep your resources high while you are away from your keyboard.

> **⚠️ Warning:** The story mode rewards have been patched to allow only one reward per stage per day to prevent exploiters from ruining the economy. Do not rely on replaying old story missions for infinite gems.

## Nightmare Circus and Item Shop

The Nightmare Circus is an endless wave mode that serves as a primary source of Gems. You can enter this mode three times a day. Reaching level 130 can net you upwards of 300 Gems in just a few minutes. 

Additionally, check the **Item Shop** every 5 minutes. It frequently stocks:
1.  **Summon Tickets:** A great way to save Gems.
2.  **Rice Cakes/Food:** Essential for "bubbling up" your units to higher levels.
3.  **Fusion Crystals:** Various rarities to help you break past level caps.

## FAQ

**Q: How many shards do I need for a Mythic unit?**

A: Currently, most high-end Mythic units like The Emperor require 50 shards. However, check the specific unit's crafting requirements in the Dungeon hub, as future updates may introduce different tiers.

**Q: Can I get a summon heroes mythic shard from standard banners?**

A: No, standard banners only drop the full Mythic unit at a 0.1% chance. Shards are specifically designed as a reward for Dungeons and special events to provide a more consistent progression path.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get Gems for summoning?**

A: The most efficient method is combining the AFK Mode (500 gems/hour) with your three daily Nightmare Circus runs. This ensures a steady flow of currency for the 30-minute rotating banners.

**Q: Does the pity system carry over to the next banner?**

A: No. Each banner has its own separate pity counter. If you are close to the 100-summon pity mark, make sure to finish your pulls before the banner rotates, or you will lose that progress.
