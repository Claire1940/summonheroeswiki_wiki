## Understanding the Summon Banners

The summon area is the heart of your progression in Summon Heroes. Unlike many other gacha games, the banners here are highly dynamic. There are typically two different packs available for summoning at any given time, and these packs rotate every 30 minutes. This fast rotation means that if you are looking for a specific unit, you must check back frequently throughout the day.

Each banner pack contains a specific distribution of units:
*   **1 Mythic Unit:** 0.1% pull chance.
*   **1 Legendary Unit:** High-tier power.
*   **2 Epic Units:** Strong mid-game staples.
*   **3 Rare Units:** Essential for utility and fusion fodder.

### Pity System and Costs

The economy of summoning is straightforward but requires discipline. A single summon costs 100 gems, while a 10-pull requires 1,000 gems. One of the most player-friendly features in 2026 is the legendary pity system. You are guaranteed a legendary unit within 100 summons. However, a critical detail to remember is that **pity is not shared between banners**. If you pull 50 times on Banner A and then switch to Banner B, your progress toward the 100-pull guarantee starts from zero on the second banner.

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Single Summon Cost** | 100 Gems |
| **10-Pull Cost** | 1,000 Gems |
| **Legendary Pity** | 100 Summons (Banner Specific) |
| **Mythic Drop Rate** | **0.1%** |
| **Banner Rotation** | Every 30 Minutes |

## The Unit Fusion System

One of the biggest mistakes new players make is enabling the "auto-sell" feature for rare and epic units. While it might seem like an easy way to clear inventory and get quick currency, it significantly hinders your long-term growth. Summon Heroes utilizes a unique fusion system that allows you to upgrade the star rarity of your units using any other unit of the same star level.

For example, if you have a four-star Dragoon and want to elevate it to five stars, you do not necessarily need another Dragoon. You can use any other four-star unit you currently own as fusion material. This flexibility makes every pull valuable, regardless of whether you intend to use the specific unit in your active party.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Never auto-sell units. Even low-tier Rares are vital "fodder" for upgrading your Legendary and Mythic carries through the fusion menu.

### Fusion Crystal Requirements

To facilitate these upgrades, you will need Fusion Crystals. These are specialized currencies used during the fusion process. While they can be earned through gameplay, the most consistent way to acquire them is through the Item Shop.

## Maximizing the Item Shop

The Item Shop in the main hub is perhaps the most underrated tool for progression. Unlike the summon banners that rotate every half hour, the shop resets every **5 minutes**. This high frequency allows dedicated players to stock up on essential supplies rapidly.

Items to prioritize in the shop:
1.  **Summon Tickets:** Occasionally appear for gold or discounted gems.
2.  **Fusion Crystals:** Different rarities appear; buy these whenever possible.
3.  **Rice Cakes & Food:** Used to "bubble up" or level your units quickly.

Regularly checking the shop every few minutes can significantly accelerate your power level without requiring heavy gem expenditures on banners.

## Daily Activities: Nightmare Circus

The Nightmare Circus is the primary daily activity for earning gems and testing your team's limits. This mode consists of an endless wave of enemies that grow progressively stronger. You are permitted **three entries per day**, so it is vital to use them when your team is at its strongest.

| Performance Metric | Estimated Reward | Time Investment |
|---|---|---|
| **Wave 50-70** | 100-150 Gems | ~2 Minutes |
| **Wave 100-130** | 300+ Gems | ~4-5 Minutes |
| **Wave 200+** | 600+ Gems | ~8+ Minutes |

The rewards scale based on how far you progress. Reaching level 130, for instance, can net you 300 gems in just a few minutes, making it one of the most time-efficient ways to fund your summons.

## Passive Progression: AFK Mode

For players who cannot be active throughout the day, the AFK mode in the main dojo is a lifesaver. By simply leaving your character in the dojo, you passively accumulate rewards over time. This is essential for maintaining a steady flow of resources for unit upgrades.

In the 2026 version of the game, players can expect approximately:
*   **500 Gems per hour**
*   Substantial amounts of Gold
*   Abundant Food supplies for leveling units

This mode ensures that even when you aren't actively battling, your account is still growing. It is highly recommended to leave your game in AFK mode whenever you are not performing manual tasks like the Nightmare Circus or managing your inventory.

## Best Early Game Units to Target

While Mythic units are the ultimate goal, their 0.1% drop rate makes them unreliable for early progression. Instead, focus on high-value Rare and Epic units that can carry you through the initial chapters.

### The Bear Tamer (Rare)
Despite being a Rare unit, the Bear Tamer is widely considered one of the best units in the game currently. His ease of acquisition means you can quickly fuse him to higher star levels. His utility and damage output punch far above his weight class, making him a mandatory pick for any starter team.

### The Mermaid (Epic)
The Mermaid excels at Area of Effect (AoE) damage. In a game dominated by wave-based combat, having a unit that can clear multiple enemies simultaneously is invaluable. A common effective strategy for new players is to run a "double Mermaid" setup alongside a Bear Tamer to balance raw AoE damage with utility.

| Unit | Rarity | Role | Why It's Good |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bear Tamer** | Rare | Utility/DPS | Easy to obtain and fuse; top-tier scaling. |
| **Mermaid** | Epic | AoE DPS | Clears waves quickly; essential for Nightmare Circus. |
| **Dragoon** | Epic | Single Target | High burst damage for boss encounters. |

## Advanced Strategy: Resource Management

Success in Summon Heroes isn't just about who you pull, but how you manage what you have. Because the game allows you to fuse different units of the same rarity, you should view your roster as a pool of potential power. 

1.  **Focus on a "Carry":** Identify your strongest unit (usually a high-star Bear Tamer or a Legendary) and dump your fusion resources into them first. A single 6-star unit is often more valuable than a full team of 4-star units.
2.  **Timing the Shop:** If you are 100 gems short of a 10-pull, check the shop or run a quick Nightmare Circus rather than waiting for the AFK timer.
3.  **Banner Patience:** Don't pull just because you have 1,000 gems. Wait for a rotation that features both a Legendary you want and a useful Rare like the Bear Tamer to maximize your "pity" value.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the pity progress carry over when the banner rotates every 30 minutes?**

A: No. Pity is tied to the specific banner pack. Since banners rotate frequently, it is best to save up 10,000 gems to ensure you can hit the 100-pull pity within a single 30-minute window if you are targeting a specific legendary.

**Q: What is the fastest way to get gems in this summon heroes guide?**

A: The fastest active method is the Nightmare Circus, which can grant hundreds of gems in minutes. The best passive method is the AFK dojo, which provides a steady 500 gems per hour.

**Q: Can I use a Rare unit to fuse into a Legendary unit?**

A: Yes, provided they are the same star rarity. If you have a five-star Rare unit and a five-star Legendary unit, you can fuse the Rare into the Legendary to make it a six-star.

**Q: How often should I check the Item Shop?**

A: Ideally every 5 minutes if you are actively playing. The high reset rate makes it the best source for Fusion Crystals and leveling food, which are necessary for maintaining a competitive team.
