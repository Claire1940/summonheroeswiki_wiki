## Top DPS Units for End-Game Clearing

When it comes to raw damage output, the **best units in summon heroes** are those that can maintain pressure from a distance or provide massive burst windows. The 2026 meta favors units that can bypass enemy frontlines or deal consistent damage to high-health bosses.

### Demon Hunter
The Demon Hunter remains a staple in almost every high-level roster. His primary strength lies in his special attack, which fires eight consecutive shots. In the end game, this ability can deal upwards of 100,000 damage in a matter of seconds. He is exceptionally consistent at the "range game," allowing you to kite enemies while dealing steady damage.

### Wind Samurai
Inspired by classic high-mobility swordsmen, the Wind Samurai is a crowd-control monster. His tornado ability is specifically designed to clear dense clusters of units, making him invaluable in the Sakura area stages. The tornado deals approximately 400% of his base damage and travels through enemies, hitting multiple targets in a single cast.

### Mermaid
While her special ability is decent, the Mermaid’s basic attack is her true claim to fame. She fires seven fish in rapid succession, locking onto targets with surprising consistency. Many players consider her more reliable than higher-rarity units like the Jester because her attack speed doesn't suffer from long wind-up animations.

## Essential Tanks and Frontline Disruptors

Survival is just as important as damage. The **best units in summon heroes** for the frontline are those that can soak up damage while providing utility to the rest of the team. In 2026, a tank's value is measured by how well they can "distract" the AI and protect your glass-cannon DPS units.

### Dragoon
The Dragoon is widely regarded as the premier starter tank. Her "Piercing Energy Spears" special attack not only deals damage but also chases enemies down, making it impossible for mobile foes to escape. She is incredibly fast for a tank, allowing her to initiate fights and draw aggro before your squishier units are targeted.

### Diver
The Diver is the definition of "tanky." While he lacks the mobility of the Dragoon, his special attack features a significant AoE component. This is crucial when he is surrounded by "trash mobs." If you find your team getting jumped by multiple melee enemies, the Diver’s ability to clear space is unmatched.

### Cyber DJ
Cyber DJ occupies a niche but effective spot in the meta. She is surprisingly durable in the end game and possesses the "Laser Dance Party" ability. This skill creates a burn zone in front of her, melting through enemy health bars. While her consistency can sometimes be hit-or-miss, her high base stats make her a safe investment for any player transitioning into mythic star levels.

## The Summoner Meta: Slimes and Bears

Summoners have seen a massive resurgence in 2026. By adding more targets to the field, summoners effectively dilute the enemy's damage output. The **best units in summon heroes** for this playstyle focus on quantity and distraction.

### Slime Summoner
The Slime Summoner is a tactical powerhouse. She launches homing slime balls and summons three melee slimes that act as secondary tanks. The AI in Summon Heroes tends to prioritize these summons, meaning your main heroes take significantly less damage. This is particularly effective against bosses with single-target high-damage attacks.

### Bear Tamer
As an honorable mention that frequently appears in top-tier lists, the Bear Tamer brings both cubs and a massive bear to the battlefield. This "army in a box" approach allows players to overwhelm stages that are technically above their power level. Summons scale well into the end game, especially when paired with buffs from units like the Mythic Reaper.

> **Warning:** Be careful when running too many melee-focused units. Even the best summoners can struggle if the entire team gets caught in a large enemy AoE (Area of Effect) attack. Always mix in ranged units like the Spellblade to balance your formation.

## Advanced Strategies for Unit Scaling

To truly maximize the **best units in summon heroes**, you must understand the progression path beyond level 100. In 2026, the focus has shifted toward specific equipment sets and star-level synergies.

1.  **Duplicate Farming:** Always keep duplicates of your Epic units. You will need them to break the level caps and reach Mythic star status.
2.  **Range Mixing:** Never run a full team of frontline attackers. Use units like **Specter**, who can thrust her sword across almost the entire map, to pick off backline threats while your tanks hold the line.
3.  **Mid-Range Support:** The **Spellblade** is a unique mid-range unit that uses homing sparks. She is perfect for filling the gap between your tanks and your furthest-ranged snipers.
4.  **Resource Allocation:** It is better to have one Mythic-star Epic unit than five base-level Legendaries. Focus your gold and upgrade materials on your core three units first.

## FAQ

**Q: Who are the best units in summon heroes for a brand-new player?**

A: For beginners, the **Dragoon** and **Demon Hunter** are the most recommended. Dragoon provides excellent survivability and speed, while Demon Hunter offers the high damage needed to clear early bosses quickly.

**Q: Is it worth upgrading Epic units to Mythic star level?**

A: Absolutely. In the 2026 meta, a Mythic-star Epic unit often has higher stats and more utility than a base Legendary unit. They are the backbone of most successful end-game teams due to their ease of acquisition.

**Q: Why is Bear Tamer considered a top-tier unit?**

A: Bear Tamer is exceptional because he provides multiple targets for the enemy to hit. His summons act as a "meat shield," allowing your main damage dealers to stay safe. This makes him one of the most consistent characters for pushing difficult content.

**Q: How does the Wind Samurai compare to other DPS units?**

A: Wind Samurai specializes in crowd control and AoE damage. While the Demon Hunter is better for single-target bosses, the Wind Samurai is superior for clearing large waves of enemies, especially in the Sakura-themed stages of the game.
