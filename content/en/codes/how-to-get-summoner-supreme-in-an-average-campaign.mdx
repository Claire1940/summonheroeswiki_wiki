## Understanding the Boon System

Before diving into the specifics of the Summoner Supreme, it is vital to understand how the power-up system works in this game. Powers in this experience are known as "Boons." These are passive bonuses or active buffs that you equip before starting a dungeon run. They are divided into two primary categories: Starter Boons and Unlockable Boons.

Starter Boons, like Summoner Supreme, are generally accessible to all players as long as they have the necessary currency. These are purchased from a specific NPC at the spawn point. Unlockable Boons, on the other hand, require you to complete difficult achievements or find hidden secrets within the campaign itself.

### The Role of the Tavern Master

The Tavern Master is the central figure for your early-game progression. Located at the spawn area, this NPC acts as the primary vendor for Starter Boons. To interact with him, you simply need to approach his stall. He offers a wide variety of buffs that can increase your health, improve your critical hit chance, or, in the case of the Summoner Supreme, bolster your summoned units.

## How to Get Summoner Supreme in An Average Campaign

To successfully acquire this boon, you must follow a straightforward but specific process. Unlike some of the more esoteric buffs that require killing specific bosses or finding hidden rooms, the Summoner Supreme is a financial investment in your character's future.

### Step 1: Accumulate Gold
The first step in **how to get summoner supreme in an average campaign** is farming enough gold. This boon costs **50 Gold**. Gold is earned by participating in dungeon runs, defeating enemies, and successfully clearing floors. Even if you fail a run, you typically keep a portion of the gold earned, allowing you to save up over time.

### Step 2: Visit the Tavern Master
Once you have at least 50 Gold in your inventory, return to the spawn area. Look for the Tavern Master near the main building. When you interact with him, a menu will appear listing all available Starter Boons.

### Step 3: Purchase and Equip
Scroll through the list until you find "Summoner Supreme." Click to purchase it. Once purchased, you must ensure it is equipped in one of your available boon slots before you start your next campaign run. 

> **Warning:** Boons must be selected before you enter the dungeon. You cannot change your equipped boons once the run has officially started.

### The Druid
The Druid is the quintessential summoner class in the game. With the ability to call upon nature spirits and animals, the Druid relies almost entirely on external units to manage the battlefield. The 30% HP boost from Summoner Supreme ensures your spirits don't vanish after a single enemy AOE (Area of Effect) attack.

### The Elementalist
While the Elementalist focuses heavily on spellcasting, certain elemental summons can provide excellent utility. Pairing Summoner Supreme with high-intelligence builds allows your elemental minions to act as secondary damage dealers while you focus on casting high-tier spells from the backline.

### Item-Based Summoners
If you happen to find rare items or scrolls that summon temporary allies, this boon still applies. This makes it a "safe" pick even if your primary class isn't a dedicated summoner, provided you plan on utilizing scrolls during your run.

## Gold Farming Tips for Summoner Supreme

Since 50 Gold can be a hurdle for absolute beginners, here are the most efficient ways to farm the currency:
*   **Target "Nobility" Boon:** This boon costs 10 Gold but gives you +150 Gold at the start of a run in exchange for -10 HP. This is the fastest way to "buy" your way into more expensive boons like Summoner Supreme.
*   **Focus on Early Rooms:** Don't feel pressured to finish the entire dungeon. Clearing the first 5-10 rooms and then intentionally ending the run (or playing safely) is often more gold-efficient than risking it all on a boss you aren't prepared for.
*   **Break Everything:** Many environment objects in the dungeon contain small amounts of gold. While it seems tedious, these small amounts add up quickly over the course of a 20-minute run.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Summoner Supreme work on summons from scrolls?**

A: Yes, the Summoner Supreme boon applies its 30% HP and 10% damage bonuses to any unit classified as a "summon," including those generated by consumable scrolls or temporary item effects.

**Q: Can I use Summoner Supreme and Potential Bunny at the same time?**

A: Absolutely. In fact, this is one of the most recommended combinations for players learning **how to get summoner supreme in an average campaign**. The boon will directly increase the stats of the bunny you receive from the Potential Bunny achievement.

**Q: Is Summoner Supreme a permanent unlock?**

A: Once you purchase a Starter Boon from the Tavern Master, it remains available for you to equip in future runs. You do not need to pay the 50 Gold every single time you play, making it a one-time investment for your account.

**Q: What happens if my summons already have high HP?**

A: The 30% bonus is multiplicative. This means the higher the base HP of your summon, the more total health they gain from the boon. This makes it even more effective in the late game when you unlock higher-tier summoning spells.
